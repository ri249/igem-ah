BioBrick Tutorial 12th - 14th of April

In the early stages of our project, two of our team members attended the Nordic BioBrick Tutorial organized by DTU Biobuilders in Copenhagen. We were the first Aalto-Helsinki team to attend this annually arranged event, but we will definitely be the first and last as this event gave us lots of good information about iGEM and many good tools to get us started with our project.

When we arrived to the DTU campus in Copenhagen, we were warmly greeted. The event started with a lecture about iGEM in general, after which we got to hear the schedule for the weekend, meet our lab group members, and attend a lecture about Human Practices in iGEM. In the evening we got the chance to discuss with the other teams about their cool projects over a pizza dinner.

The second day of the Nordic BioBrick Tutorial was all about the BioBrick Tutorial, which we got to do in mixed groups formed of students from different Universities from the Nordic countries. In the lab, we made a basic assembly with BioBricks, and completed a transformation into E. coli DH5Î± cells. This was a very useful experience as we got a good introduction to BioBricks while doing it. Between the lab sessions at DTU, we had lunch, took a group photo, and attended a workshop about pitching. Before plating the transformants in the evening, we attended lectures about different cloning strategies, and iGEM Wiki page. After a successful day of lab work, we went to have dinner in the DTU building 101 cafeteria. Here we again got the chance to socialize with the other teams and ask them about their work and plans for the summer.

On the final day of the Nordic BioBrick Tutorial we got to listen to an iGEM alumni panel at DTU, and see the results of our hard labwork, which turned out great! After the final wrap-up session, we then headed to the city center to have dinner with the iGEM team from Oslo, after which we returned home to Finland to tell about our great trip to the rest of the team.
