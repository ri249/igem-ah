COLLABORATIONS

BioBrick Tutorial 12th - 14th of April

In the early stages of our project, two of our team members got the chance to attend the Nordic BioBrick Tutorial organized by DTU Biobuilders in Copenhagen. We were the first Aalto-Helsinki team to attend this annually arranged event, and we will recommend all the future teams to attend it as this event gave us lots of good information about iGEM and many beneficial tools to get us started with our project, which will be very beneficial for all future teams also.

On Friday morning we arrived to the DTU campus in Copenhagen, where we were warmly greeted. The event started with a lecture about iGEM in general, after which we got to hear the schedule for the weekend, meet our lab group members, and attend a lecture about Human Practices in iGEM. In the evening we got the chance to discuss with the other teams about their cool projects over a pizza dinner.

The second day of the Nordic BioBrick Tutorial was all about the BioBrick Tutorial, which we did in mixed groups formed of students from different Universities from the Nordic countries. In the lab, we made a basic assembly with BioBricks, and completed a transformation into E. coli DH5Î± cells. This was a very useful experience as we got a good introduction to BioBricks while doing it. Between the lab sessions at DTU, we had lunch, took a group photo, and attended a workshop about pitching. Before plating the transformants in the evening, we attended lectures about different cloning strategies, and iGEM Wiki page. After a successful day of lab work, we went to have dinner in the DTU building 101 cafeteria. Here we again got the chance to socialize with the other teams and ask them about their work and plans for the summer.

On the final day of the Nordic BioBrick Tutorial we got to listen to an iGEM alumni panel at DTU, and see the results of our hard lab work, which turned out great! After the final wrap-up session, we then headed to the city center to have dinner with the iGEM team from Oslo, after which we returned home to Finland to tell about our great trip to the rest of the team.



Nordic iGEM Conference 17th - 19th of May

In mid-May, before we even had started working full-time with our project, we attended our second iGEM collaboration event. Only a month after the Nordic BioBrick Tutorial it was time to return to Copenhagen, but this time for The Nordic iGEM Conference (NiC). This time the size of the Aalto-Helsinki Copenhagen delegation grew to seven people attending the occasion, so almost all of the team members were able to participate.

The whole team was excited about attending the conference as we would see with what exciting ideas the other teams had started to work with and how their projects were proceeding.
The conference started with a warm welcome by our hosts, the stellar iGEM team Copenhagen. The first workshop was held after a welcoming ceremony and the subject was science communication. The interesting workshop was followed by dinner and the first night culminated into a delightful scavenger hunt with mixed teams of all the attending iGEM teams. The scavenger hunt was an excellent opportunity to get to know the other teams!

The main events of the weekend were the Mini-Jamboree and the poster sessions on Saturday. The pressure was on: Our team had to present our project by pitching in front of the audience and the judges and present our poster at the poster session. Being the second to last team to present, we were nervous after hearing all the impressive and well-developed presentations and project plans one after the other. Despite all this, our team delivered our pitch and the poster presentation well and we got some good feedback from other teams!

The Saturday continued with a poster session, workshops and even a Hackathon. Afterward, we enjoyed dinner and continued the night together with the other iGEM teams in Copenhagen.

On Sunday, after the official program (which included a very useful modeling workshop) our team ended the weekend with a team meeting in which we reflected back on what we had learned during the conference (and we did learn a lot) and what could be improved to the next conference weâre attending. The NiC was very beneficial experience for the team also regarding the possible future collaborations with other iGEM teams.



European iGEM meetup in Paris on the 26th - 27th of July

One of the highlights of the summer was the European iGEM meetup in Paris on the 26th - 27th of July. The two-day long conference was organized by four parisian teams: Pasteur Paris, Ionis Paris, GO Paris Saclay and Evry Paris Saclay at the Institut Pasteur. Almost 20 teams all around Europe attended the meetup to present their projects in forms of posters and presentations to get feedback and insights from the experienced jury.

This time we came to the conference with more confidence compared to the last time we attended a conference in Copenhagen â knowing that we had put in a lot of effort to redo both the presentation and our poster. We were happy to see how much we had gone forward since the last international conference. At the same time, we returned home with motivation and know-how about how to do even better in our next conference in Boston!

In addition to getting some very valued feedback from judges on our presentation and poster and learn from other teams great presentations and posters, we also had the chance to enjoy beautiful Paris for a bit before and after the conference.

European iGEM meetup in Paris on the 26th - 27th of July
